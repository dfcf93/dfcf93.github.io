Hi there, I'm Daniel Campos!

I am a technologist,winemaker,dad,glad-scientist in Urbana Illinois.

I am pursing a PhD in Computer Science at UIUC where I will work under Prof. Heng Ji as part of the Blender Lab.

I am researching how to make NLP and transformer models more efficient with the good folks at Neural Magic.

My primary interests lie in the intersection of efficient models, natural language processing and information understanding systems(IR, IE, etc.)

I have worked to build MS MARCO and the TREC Deep Learning Track and am always interested in making new and interesting datasets.

In a past life I worked at Microsoft as a PM for Bing focused on Applied Science and Metrics.

I am part of the CLMBR at the University of Washington where I am a Masters candidate in Computational Linguistics. My advisor is Shane Steinert-Threlkeld and I am in the final stages of my thesis focusing Curriculum Learning applied to language modeling.

I love to get nerdy about wine, computers, the nature of intelligence, and any sport with a board.
